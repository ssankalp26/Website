I am a graduate student in the Department of Aerospace Engineering at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, pursuing my passion for creating sustainable solutions for air travel. My goal is to help the aviation industry develop a multi-pronged approach to achieve the net zero carbon goal set by the industry.

Working on innovative solutions for electrified urban air mobility, I am part of the Laboratory for Electric Aircraft Design and Sustainability (L.E.A.D.S.) under the mentorship of Dr. Matthew Clarke at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. We are building a state-of-the-art lab to perform some really exciting tests on batteries that will be used in electric aircraft. In parallel, we are also developing a comprehensive computational tool, RCAIDE, which aids in a system-level understanding of the behavior of all types of aircraft under varying conditions.

Before I joined UIUC, I worked at Collins Aerospace with their technology team on various projects such as SpaceChiller™ and Pothos™. Additionally, I dealt with electronic components packaging and thermal efficiency improvement in adherence to DO-160 standards.
