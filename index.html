I am a graduate student in the Department of Aerospace Engineering at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, pursuing my passion for creating sustainable solutions for air travel. My research focuses on developing a multi-pronged approach to help the aviation industry achieve its ambitious net-zero carbon emissions goal.

As part of the Laboratory for Electric Aircraft Design and Sustainability (L.E.A.D.S.), under the mentorship of Dr. Matthew Clarke, I'm working on innovative solutions for electrified urban air mobility. Our team is establishing a state-of-the-art laboratory to conduct cutting-edge tests on batteries for electric aircraft. In parallel, we're developing RCAIDE, a comprehensive computational tool that provides system-level analysis of various aircraft types under diverse operating conditions.
Before joining UIUC, I contributed to groundbreaking projects at Collins Aerospace, collaborating with their technology team on SpaceChiller™, an innovative cabin cooling system, and Pothos™, a pioneering approach to sensory comfort in aircraft cabins. Additionally, I gained valuable experience in electronic components packaging and thermal efficiency improvement, adhering to DO-160 standards.
This unique blend of academic research and industry experience has equipped me with a comprehensive understanding of the challenges and opportunities in sustainable aviation. I'm committed to pushing the boundaries of aerospace engineering to create an environmentally friendly future for air travel.
